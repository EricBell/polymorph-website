What it says (simple)
This paper surveyed about 100 small businesses and interviewed owners to see what happens when they use AI. It finds AI can boost productivity by automating routine tasks, improve customer service, cut some operating costs, and spur small-scale innovation (new services or ways of working). It also flags common problems: limited skills, worries about privacy/security, and the trouble of fitting new tools into small teams.

Why it matters to your business (simple)
For a marketer, that means real, usable wins: faster content and ad creation, better customer responses, and fewer hours spent on boring admin. The study shows these are not just theoretical — small firms in the survey reported measurable gains. It also warns that you won’t get overnight magic: you need a plan for skills and data privacy so the gains stick.

Recommended next steps (simple)
- Pick one quick win: automate weekly social posts, make 2 email templates with AI, or add a simple FAQ chatbot.  
- Run a 4–6 week pilot and track time saved, response time, and any lift in leads or conversions.  
- If you don’t have the skills in-house, partner with a vendor or freelancer for the pilot.  
- Make sure customer data is handled safely (basic privacy checklist: limit data used, password-protect accounts, check vendor policies).
